# Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Charter

## Standard Definitions

**Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation** means the broader family legacy mission and the intended future trust structure created to build, protect, organize, and preserve family assets for current and future generations. Unless a trust agreement has been legally created and executed, the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation refers to the mission and planning framework only—not an existing legal trust or charitable entity.

**Justice McNeal LLC** means the current Georgia single-member limited liability company owned and controlled by Justin McNeal. Justice McNeal LLC currently serves as the operating and administrative entity that collects contributions, maintains records, manages approved expenses, and supports the early mission stage of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation.

**Intended future trust** means the family trust that the family plans to create in the future, currently referred to by the working name Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation. The final legal name, form, and terms must be determined through professionally drafted trust documents and attorney review.

**Contributor** means a person who voluntarily contributes funds through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission. A Contributor is not a legal owner, LLC member, trustee, beneficiary, manager, or decision-maker unless separate legally binding documents say otherwise.

**Active Legacy Contributor** means a Contributor who maintains an active recurring monthly contribution through the approved contribution system under the Contribution Policy. This status describes mission participation only. It does not create ownership, equity, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

**Family roles / appointed positions** mean responsibilities assigned to assist with mission activities, events, records, outreach, or planning. Unless created by legally binding documents, these roles do not confer ownership, management authority over Justice McNeal LLC, or trustee authority over any future trust.

**Separate legacy entities** include Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, future property-holding LLCs, and other businesses or holding entities. Each entity has its own purpose and records. They are not automatically combined unless formally connected by legal documents. In the intended future structure, the intended future trust may own or control some or all of these entities.

**Planning document notice:** This document describes current intentions and operating concepts only. It is not legal, tax, investment, estate planning, or financial advice, and it does not create binding legal rights by itself.

## 1. Purpose of This Charter

This charter explains the purpose, values, current structure, and intended future direction of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

The purpose of this document is to create a clear foundation for how the family intends to build, protect, organize, and pass down wealth across generations. This charter is meant to explain the mission, guiding principles, family contribution philosophy, current operating structure, and long-term vision for the intended future trust and related family-owned entities.

Justice McNeal LLC is currently the primary legal vehicle being used during the early mission stage administered through Justice McNeal LLC. In the future, the family intends to create a properly drafted intended future trust—Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation, the intended future trust structure—that may own or control Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, property-holding LLCs, investment assets, land, and other family legacy assets.

This document is not intended to replace an LLC operating agreement, trust agreement, tax advice, legal advice, financial advice, or professional estate planning. It is an internal planning and family alignment document meant to guide discussion, record intentions, and help prepare for professional legal and tax review.

## 2. Origin of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Mission

The Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission began with the shared intention of building something larger than any one individual. It was created from the belief that family members working together, contributing consistently, and thinking long-term can create opportunities that last beyond the current generation.

The early mission stage was started by Justin McNeal, Yolanda McNeal, Cedric Justice, Shakeytha McNeal, Shawana McNeal, and Celedric McNeal in Georgia.

Justice McNeal LLC currently serves as the primary legal and operating vehicle during the early mission stage administered through Justice McNeal LLC. Justice McNeal LLC is a Georgia single-member LLC owned and controlled by Justin McNeal. Although the LLC is currently owned and controlled by Justin McNeal, the broader purpose of the mission is family-centered and focused on creating a long-term structure for generational wealth, asset ownership, family education, and legacy planning.

The mission began with the understanding that wealth is not only built through income, but through organization, discipline, ownership, education, and cooperation. The goal is to build an organizing structure that can eventually support a properly drafted intended future trust, related family-owned entities, investments, land, businesses, and other assets intended to benefit future generations.

## 3. Mission Statement

The mission of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation is to build, protect, and organize family wealth for current and future generations through disciplined contributions, long-term investing, asset ownership, education, and family cooperation.

This mission is rooted in the belief that generational wealth is built over time through consistency, stewardship, financial education, and shared family responsibility. The goal is not only to accumulate assets, but to create a lasting structure that can support opportunity, stability, ownership, and legacy for future generations.

## 4. Core Values

The Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission is guided by core values that help define how decisions should be made, how assets should be protected, and how the family should work together over time.

### Generational Wealth

The purpose of this mission is to build something that can benefit not only the current generation, but future generations as well. The focus is on long-term ownership, stability, and legacy.

### Family Unity

This mission is built on the belief that family cooperation can create opportunities that are difficult to build alone. Unity does not mean everyone will always agree, but it does mean decisions should be guided by respect, communication, and the shared mission.

### Transparency

Clear communication and accurate recordkeeping are important to maintaining trust. Contributions, expenses, decisions, and progress should be documented in a way that promotes accountability and understanding.

### Discipline

Generational wealth requires consistency, patience, and responsible decision-making. This mission should prioritize long-term progress over short-term wants.

### Long-Term Thinking

Decisions should be made with future generations in mind. The goal is not quick profit, but lasting value, asset ownership, and protection of the family legacy.

### Stewardship

Anyone trusted with responsibility in this mission should act as a steward of the family legacy. This means managing assets, opportunities, and decisions with care, responsibility, and respect for the mission.

### Education

Financial education, business knowledge, ownership principles, and family learning are important parts of the mission. The goal is not only to build wealth, but to help the family understand how wealth is created, protected, and passed down.

### Accountability

Family members who serve in appointed roles or manage assigned responsibilities should be accountable to the mission and to the family. Final decision-making authority today rests with Justin McNeal as owner and managing member of Justice McNeal LLC. Future decision-making will follow formal trust and governance documents once legally created. Responsibility should be handled with honesty, consistency, and care.

### No Entitlement

Participation in the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission should not create a sense of entitlement to money, assets, control, or personal benefit. The purpose is to build and protect the legacy, not to create automatic personal claims.

### Protection of the Legacy

The family legacy should be protected from misuse, poor planning, unnecessary risk, conflict, and decisions that benefit one person unfairly at the expense of the broader mission.

## 8. Family Contributions

Contributions are voluntary funds contributed through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

These contributions are intended to help support the long-term mission of building generational wealth, investing for the future, acquiring and protecting assets, supporting family education, and preparing for the future creation of the intended future trust.

Unless otherwise documented in a legally binding agreement, contributions do not create ownership, equity, LLC membership rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, voting rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

Contributions may be made on a recurring or one-time basis. The amount and frequency of contributions may vary by person and may change over time. Contributors who maintain an active recurring monthly contribution through the approved system may be recognized as Active Legacy Contributors under the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Contribution Policy. This status describes mission participation only and does not create ownership or legal rights.

Contribution records may be maintained for transparency, organization, and accountability. However, tracking contributions does not mean that contributors own a percentage of Justice McNeal LLC, the intended future trust, investment accounts, land, businesses, or other family legacy assets.

Contributions should be understood as voluntary support for the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission, not as an investment contract, loan, membership purchase, subscription for ownership, or guarantee of future payment.

For detailed contribution rules, tracking procedures, Active Legacy Contributor status, refunds, and payment handling, see the *Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Contribution Policy*.

The treatment of contributions should be reviewed by a qualified CPA, attorney, or other appropriate professional to ensure that contributions are documented and handled properly.

## 9. No Guaranteed Returns

The Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission is intended to support long-term wealth-building, asset ownership, education, and family legacy planning. However, no contribution creates a guaranteed financial return.

Contributors should not expect a guaranteed repayment, profit, dividend, distribution, ownership interest, or financial benefit because of their contribution.

Any funds used for investing or asset-building may increase or decrease in value. Investments, businesses, land, property, and other assets may carry risk, including the possible loss of value.

The purpose of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission is not to provide short-term returns or individual payouts. The purpose is to build and protect a long-term family structure that may benefit current and future generations over time.

Any future distributions, benefits, use of assets, or family support should follow the rules of the applicable legal documents, including any future trust agreement, LLC operating agreement, family governance policy, or written approval process adopted at that time.

## 11. Restricted or Prohibited Uses of Funds

Funds connected to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission should not be used for purposes that conflict with the mission, values, or long-term goals of the family legacy structure.

Restricted or prohibited uses of funds include:

- Personal spending unrelated to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission
- Personal bills, lifestyle expenses, or entertainment unrelated to the mission
- Unapproved withdrawals
- Loans to family members without a written policy and documented approval
- Payments or benefits that unfairly favor one person over the broader Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission
- High-risk speculative trading
- Gambling, lottery-style investing, or betting
- Purchases made without proper documentation
- Commingling funds with unrelated personal accounts or personal spending
- Using funds for personal emergencies without a written policy and approval process
- Business expenses unrelated to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission
- Political donations or political activity
- Illegal, unethical, or misleading activity
- Any use of funds that creates unnecessary risk, conflict, confusion, or harm to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission

Funds should be handled with discipline, transparency, and accountability. Any use of funds that is unclear, unusual, or potentially personal should be documented and reviewed before funds are spent.

As the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission develops, more specific restrictions may be created through a trust agreement, LLC operating agreement, contribution policy, investment policy, family governance policy, or written approval process.
