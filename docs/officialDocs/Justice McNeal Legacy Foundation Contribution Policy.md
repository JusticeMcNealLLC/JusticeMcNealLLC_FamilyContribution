# Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Contribution Policy

## Administered Through Justice McNeal LLC

## Standard Definitions

**Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation** means the broader family legacy mission and the intended future trust structure created to build, protect, organize, and preserve family assets for current and future generations. Unless a trust agreement has been legally created and executed, the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation refers to the mission and planning framework only—not an existing legal trust or charitable entity.

**Justice McNeal LLC** means the current Georgia single-member limited liability company owned and controlled by Justin McNeal. Justice McNeal LLC currently serves as the operating and administrative entity that collects contributions, maintains records, manages approved expenses, and supports the early mission stage of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation.

**Intended future trust** means the family trust that the family plans to create in the future, currently referred to by the working name Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation. The final legal name, form, and terms must be determined through professionally drafted trust documents and attorney review.

**Contributions** means voluntary funds contributed through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

**Contributor** means a person who voluntarily contributes funds through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission. A Contributor is not a legal owner, LLC member, trustee, beneficiary, manager, or decision-maker unless separate legally binding documents say otherwise.

**Active Legacy Contributor** means a Contributor who maintains an active recurring monthly contribution through the approved contribution system under this policy. This status describes mission participation only. It does not create ownership, equity, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

**Family roles / appointed positions** mean responsibilities assigned to assist with mission activities, events, records, outreach, or planning. Unless created by legally binding documents, these roles do not confer ownership, management authority over Justice McNeal LLC, or trustee authority over any future trust.

**Separate legacy entities** include Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, future property-holding LLCs, and other businesses or holding entities. Each entity has its own purpose and records. They are not automatically combined unless formally connected by legal documents.

**Planning document notice:** This document describes current intentions and operating concepts only. It is not legal, tax, investment, estate planning, or financial advice, and it does not create binding legal rights by itself.

## 1. Purpose of This Policy

This policy explains how contributions are made, tracked, handled, and understood when contributed through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

The purpose of this policy is to create clarity around contributions and help prevent confusion about ownership, refunds, financial returns, access, participation, and contributor status.

For the broader mission, values, and family contribution philosophy, see the *Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Charter*.

This policy is an internal planning and family alignment document. It is not intended to replace legal advice, tax advice, financial advice, a trust agreement, an LLC operating agreement, or any professionally prepared legal document.

## 2. What Contributions Are

Contributions are voluntary funds contributed through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

These funds are intended to help support the long-term mission of building generational wealth, organizing family resources, investing for the future, supporting family education, preparing for the intended future trust, and creating opportunities for current and future generations.

Contributions may help support approved uses connected to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission, including investing, administrative costs, legal and tax planning, website costs, family events connected to the mission, future trust setup, and other approved legacy-related purposes.

## 3. Contributor Status

A person who contributes to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission through Justice McNeal LLC is a **Contributor**.

Contributors who maintain an active recurring monthly contribution through the approved system may be recognized as **Active Legacy Contributors**.

Contributor status is used to describe participation in the mission. It does not create legal ownership in Justice McNeal LLC, the intended future trust, investment accounts, land, businesses, property, or other family legacy assets.

A Contributor is not automatically a legal LLC member, trustee, beneficiary, manager, advisor, voter, or decision-maker.

## 4. How People Contribute

Contributions may be made through approved payment methods provided by Justice McNeal LLC, including the website and Stripe payment system.

Contributors may contribute monthly on a recurring basis. If available, contributors may also make a one-time contribution.

Monthly recurring contributions may be used to establish Active Legacy Contributor status. One-time contributions may be accepted, but a one-time contribution does not automatically establish Active Legacy Contributor status.

Contributors are not permitted to prepay multiple months in advance unless a written exception is approved. This helps keep monthly participation, contribution records, and Active Legacy Contributor status clear and organized.

## 5. Whether Contributions Are Required or Voluntary

Participation in the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission is voluntary. No person is required to contribute.

**Active Legacy Contributor** is an optional status for those who choose recurring monthly support through the approved contribution system. If a person chooses not to contribute, stops contributing, cancels their recurring contribution, or has repeated failed payments, they may lose Active Legacy Contributor status and any access, updates, benefits, or participation privileges connected to that status.

Any access, updates, events, or participation privileges connected to Active Legacy Contributor status are mission-participation benefits only. They are not financial returns, profit sharing, or ownership interests.

Stopping contributions does not create a right to repayment, refund, ownership, equity, LLC membership, withdrawal, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, or financial return.

## 6. Whether Contributions Create Ownership

Contributions do not create legal ownership in Justice McNeal LLC, the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission, the intended future trust, investment accounts, land, businesses, property, or other family legacy assets.

Contributions do not create equity, LLC membership rights, voting rights, management rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

Contribution records may be maintained for transparency and accountability, but tracking contributions does not mean that Contributors own a percentage of Justice McNeal LLC, the intended future trust, investments, land, businesses, property, or other assets.

## 7. Refund Policy

Contributions are generally non-refundable.

Once a contribution is made, it may be used for approved purposes connected to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission. Contributors should only contribute funds they are comfortable giving in support of the mission.

Refunds should not be expected unless a written exception is approved or required by applicable law, payment processor rules, or professional legal/tax guidance.

## 8. Stripe and Payment Processing

Justice McNeal LLC may use Stripe or another approved payment processor to collect recurring and one-time contributions.

Stripe may process contributor payments, maintain payment records, charge processing fees, and transfer funds to the connected bank or financial account.

Payment processor fees, failed payment fees, dispute fees, chargeback fees, or related processing costs may be treated as administrative expenses connected to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

Contributors are responsible for keeping their payment information current if they wish to maintain Active Legacy Contributor status.

## 9. Movement of Funds

Funds may move from Stripe to a business bank account, such as Bluevine, and then to an investment account, such as Fidelity.

Funds may also move from Stripe directly to Fidelity if that process is available, approved, and properly documented.

All movement of funds should be tracked clearly so that contributions, deposits, transfers, investments, and expenses can be reviewed and understood over time.

## 10. Recordkeeping

Justice McNeal LLC should maintain records for all contribution-related activity connected to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

Records may include:

- Contributor names
- Contribution dates
- Contribution amounts
- One-time contributions
- Monthly recurring contributions
- Failed or canceled payments
- Stripe or payment processor records
- Bank deposit records
- Transfers to investment accounts
- Investment deposits
- Administrative expenses
- Refunds or exceptions, if any
- Contributor status changes

Records are maintained for transparency, organization, and accountability. Records do not create ownership, equity, voting rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

## 11. Contribution Reports

Contribution reports may be shared with contributors or family participants to promote transparency and accountability.

Reports may include summaries of:

- Total contributions received
- Total expenses paid
- Funds transferred to bank or investment accounts
- Current investment or account balances
- Approved uses of funds
- Major decisions or updates
- Progress toward Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation goals

Contribution reports are for informational purposes only. Reports do not create ownership rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, voting rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

## 12. Website Language

The website should use clear language that reflects the purpose of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission and avoids confusion about ownership or investment returns.

Website language should avoid terms that may imply legal ownership, guaranteed returns, equity, profit-sharing, or investment contracts unless approved by an attorney or other qualified professional.

Preferred language may include:

- Family legacy contribution
- Monthly legacy contribution
- Voluntary family contribution
- Active Legacy Contributor
- Support the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission
- Contributed through Justice McNeal LLC
- No ownership interest created
- No guaranteed return
- No repayment or withdrawal right

The website should avoid unclear or misleading language such as:

- Investment subscription
- Ownership contribution
- Equity contribution
- Profit share
- Guaranteed return
- LLC member, unless referring to a legal LLC owner
- Trust beneficiary, unless legally defined by a trust document

## 13. If Someone Stops Contributing

If a person stops contributing monthly, cancels their recurring contribution, or has repeated failed payments, they may lose Active Legacy Contributor status.

Losing Active Legacy Contributor status may result in loss of access to specific benefits, updates, events, resources, or participation privileges connected to that status. These are mission-participation benefits only and are not financial returns, profit sharing, or ownership interests.

Stopping contributions does not create a right to repayment, refund, ownership, equity, LLC membership, withdrawal, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, or financial return.

A person who stops contributing may be able to restart contributions later, subject to the rules and approval process in place at that time.

## 14. Before and After the Trust Is Created

Before the intended future trust is legally created and funded, Justice McNeal LLC currently serves as the primary operating and contribution-collection entity for the early mission stage administered through Justice McNeal LLC.

During this pre-trust planning stage, contributions are voluntary funds contributed through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission but do not create ownership in Justice McNeal LLC or any intended future trust.

After a properly drafted intended future trust is legally created, contribution rules may be updated to align with the trust agreement, LLC operating agreement, tax guidance, and professional legal advice.

The intended future trust may create new rules for how contributions are received, tracked, used, invested, reported, restricted, or transferred. Any future changes should be documented in writing and communicated clearly to Contributors.

## 15. Professional Review

This policy should be reviewed by a qualified attorney, CPA, or other appropriate professional before being treated as final legal, tax, financial, or governance guidance.

Professional review may be needed to determine:

- How contributions should be classified
- Whether contributions should be treated as gifts
- How Stripe payments should be recorded
- How funds should move between Stripe, Bluevine, Fidelity, and any other accounts
- How contribution records should be maintained
- Whether contributor language creates legal or tax concerns
- How this policy should change once a trust is created
- Whether securities, investment adviser, private fund, or other rules may apply
- Whether the name "Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation" should be used as the legal trust name, public mission name, or both
