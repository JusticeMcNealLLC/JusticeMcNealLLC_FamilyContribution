# Justice McNeal LLC Operating Notes

## Single-Member LLC Structure Draft

## Standard Definitions

**Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation** means the broader family legacy mission and the intended future trust structure created to build, protect, organize, and preserve family assets for current and future generations. Unless a trust agreement has been legally created and executed, the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation refers to the mission and planning framework only—not an existing legal trust or charitable entity.

**Justice McNeal LLC** means the current Georgia single-member limited liability company owned and controlled by Justin McNeal. Justice McNeal LLC currently serves as the operating and administrative entity that collects contributions, maintains records, manages approved expenses, and supports the early mission stage of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation.

**Intended future trust** means the family trust that the family plans to create in the future, currently referred to by the working name Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation. The final legal name, form, and terms must be determined through professionally drafted trust documents and attorney review.

**Contributor** means a person who voluntarily contributes funds through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission. A Contributor is not a legal owner, LLC member, trustee, beneficiary, manager, or decision-maker unless separate legally binding documents say otherwise.

**Active Legacy Contributor** means a Contributor who maintains an active recurring monthly contribution through the approved contribution system under the Contribution Policy. This status describes mission participation only. It does not create ownership, equity, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

**Family roles / appointed positions** mean responsibilities assigned to assist with mission activities, events, records, outreach, or planning. Unless created by legally binding documents, these roles do not confer ownership, management authority over Justice McNeal LLC, or trustee authority over any future trust.

**Separate legacy entities** include Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, future property-holding LLCs, and other businesses or holding entities. Each entity has its own purpose and records. They are not automatically combined unless formally connected by legal documents. In the intended future structure, the intended future trust may own or control some or all of these entities.

**Planning document notice:** This document describes current intentions and operating concepts only. It is not legal, tax, investment, estate planning, or financial advice, and it does not create binding legal rights by itself.

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document explains the current operating structure of Justice McNeal LLC.

Justice McNeal LLC is a Georgia single-member LLC owned and controlled by Justin McNeal. This document is intended to describe how the LLC currently operates, how it supports the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission, and how it may later fit into the intended future trust structure.

This document is an internal planning and operating document. It is not intended to replace a professionally drafted LLC operating agreement, legal advice, tax advice, financial advice, estate planning advice, or any future trust agreement.

## 2. Current Legal Status

Justice McNeal LLC is organized as a Georgia limited liability company.

Justice McNeal LLC is a Georgia single-member LLC owned and controlled by Justin McNeal. Justin McNeal is the owner and managing member of the LLC.

Until formal legal documents provide otherwise, Justin McNeal has final decision-making authority as owner and managing member of Justice McNeal LLC over the LLC’s operations, website, accounts, records, contributions, expenses, investments, contracts, and business decisions.

## 3. Relationship to Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation

Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation means the broader family legacy mission and intended future trust structure created to build, protect, organize, and preserve family assets for current and future generations.

Justice McNeal LLC currently serves as the operating and administrative entity used to support the early mission stage administered through Justice McNeal LLC.

Until the intended future trust is legally created, funded, and connected to the LLC, Justice McNeal LLC remains owned and controlled by Justin McNeal.

## 4. Business Purpose

The purpose of Justice McNeal LLC is to support the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission by helping organize, administer, and manage activities connected to family legacy building.

The LLC may be used for purposes including:

* Collecting and tracking family legacy contributions
* Operating and maintaining the website
* Managing payment processing systems
* Maintaining records and reports
* Paying approved administrative expenses
* Supporting long-term investing activity
* Supporting legal, tax, accounting, and trust planning
* Supporting family events connected to the mission
* Supporting future land, property, business, or asset ownership planning
* Coordinating professional review and documentation

Justice McNeal LLC should be operated in a way that supports long-term family legacy goals, transparency, accountability, and responsible stewardship.

## 5. Ownership

Justice McNeal LLC is owned and controlled by Justin McNeal as a single-member LLC.

Family members, Contributors, advisors, event coordinators, role-holders, or participants do not receive ownership in Justice McNeal LLC unless ownership is formally granted through legally binding documents.

Contributions made through Justice McNeal LLC do not create ownership, equity, LLC membership rights, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, management rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

## 6. Management Authority

Justin McNeal currently serves as the owner and managing member of Justice McNeal LLC.

As managing member, Justin McNeal currently has authority to manage the LLC’s operations, website, bank accounts, payment processing accounts, investment-related accounts, records, expenses, contracts, professional services, and administrative decisions.

Justin McNeal may seek input from family members, Contributors, advisors, or appointed role-holders before making major decisions. However, unless otherwise documented in a legally binding agreement, final decision-making authority remains with Justin McNeal as owner and managing member of Justice McNeal LLC.

## 8. Contributions Collected Through the LLC

Justice McNeal LLC may collect voluntary contributions through approved systems such as the website, Stripe, bank transfers, or other approved payment methods.

Contributions are voluntary funds contributed through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

Contributions should be handled according to the *Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Contribution Policy* administered through Justice McNeal LLC. For full contribution rules and procedures, see that policy. Contributor status, including Active Legacy Contributor, is defined there.

Contributions do not create ownership, equity, LLC membership rights, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial returns. Contribution records may be maintained for transparency and accountability, but contribution records do not create ownership in Justice McNeal LLC or any intended future trust.

## 9. Bank, Payment, and Investment Accounts

Justice McNeal LLC may maintain bank accounts, payment processor accounts, investment-related accounts, or other financial accounts necessary to support its operations and the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

Funds may move from Stripe to a business bank account, such as Bluevine, and then to an investment account, such as Fidelity.

Funds may also move from Stripe directly to Fidelity if that process is available, approved, and properly documented.

All accounts, transfers, deposits, expenses, and investment-related activity should be tracked clearly.
