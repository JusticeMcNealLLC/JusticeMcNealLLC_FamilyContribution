# Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Structure

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document explains how the pieces of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission fit together — who owns what today, how contributions flow, and how the family intends to organize entities and assets over time.

The purpose of this document is to provide a clear, practical overview of the current pre-trust structure and the intended future structure. It is meant to help family members, Contributors, and advisors understand the relationships between Justice McNeal LLC, the broader mission, and the planned entities and assets that may eventually be owned or controlled by the intended future trust.

This document is an internal planning and alignment document only. It is not a legally created trust agreement, an LLC operating agreement, tax advice, legal advice, financial advice, or estate planning advice. It does not create binding legal rights by itself.

## 2. Standard Definitions

**Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation** means the broader family legacy mission and the intended future trust structure created to build, protect, organize, and preserve family assets for current and future generations. Unless a trust agreement has been legally created and executed, the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation refers to the mission and planning framework only—not an existing legal trust or charitable entity.

**Justice McNeal LLC** means the current Georgia single-member limited liability company owned and controlled by Justin McNeal. Justice McNeal LLC currently serves as the operating and administrative entity that collects contributions, maintains records, manages approved expenses, and supports the early mission stage of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation.

**Intended future trust** means the family trust that the family plans to create in the future, currently referred to by the working name Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation. The final legal name, form, and terms must be determined through professionally drafted trust documents and attorney review.

**Contributor** means a person who voluntarily contributes funds through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission. A Contributor is not a legal owner, LLC member, trustee, beneficiary, manager, or decision-maker unless separate legally binding documents say otherwise.

**Active Legacy Contributor** means a Contributor who maintains an active recurring monthly contribution through the approved contribution system under the Contribution Policy. This status describes mission participation only. It does not create ownership, equity, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

**Separate legacy entities** include Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, future property-holding LLCs, and other businesses or holding entities. Each entity has its own purpose and records. They are not automatically combined unless formally connected by legal documents. In the intended future structure, the intended future trust may own or control some or all of these entities.

**Planning document notice:** This document describes current intentions and operating concepts only. It is not legal, tax, investment, estate planning, or financial advice, and it does not create binding legal rights by itself.

## 3. Current Structure

Justice McNeal LLC is a Georgia single-member LLC owned and controlled by Justin McNeal.

During the early mission stage administered through Justice McNeal LLC, the LLC currently serves as the operating, administrative, and contribution-collection entity for the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission. This includes collecting voluntary contributions, maintaining records, managing the website, paying approved expenses, and supporting mission-related activities.

The intended future trust—Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation, the intended future trust structure—does not legally exist yet unless and until it is professionally drafted, legally created, funded, and connected to the appropriate entities and assets.

Until that occurs, Justice McNeal LLC remains owned and controlled by Justin McNeal, and final decision-making authority for the LLC rests with Justin McNeal as owner and managing member unless formal legal documents provide otherwise.

Contributors support the mission through Justice McNeal LLC but do not receive ownership, equity, LLC membership, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, voting rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial returns because of their contributions.

## 5. Intended Future Structure

The intended future structure is for Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation, as the intended future trust structure, to become the primary ownership or control structure for family legacy assets once it is legally created.

The intended future trust may own or control multiple entities and assets that serve different purposes within the family legacy structure. This may include Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, property-holding LLCs, investment accounts, land, future businesses, brands, digital assets, and other legacy assets connected to the mission.

Justice McNeal LLC may continue to serve as the contribution-collection, administration, website, records, and mission-related events entity under the broader intended future trust structure, if that structure is adopted through proper legal documents.

Nothing in this document assumes that the intended future trust has already been created, funded, or connected to any entity or asset. All future structure descriptions are planning concepts only until formal legal documents are in place.

## 7. Entity Roles

### Justice McNeal LLC

Justice McNeal LLC is intended to serve as the operating and administrative entity for the early mission stage and, in the future, may continue to handle contribution collection, website operations, records, mission administration, and mission-related events under the broader intended future trust structure.

### Karry Kraze

Karry Kraze is a separate e-commerce business intended to generate business revenue. In the future, some or all of Karry Kraze's ownership, profits, distributions, or assets may be directed to or owned by the intended future trust, subject to proper legal, tax, business, and estate planning guidance.

Karry Kraze should remain operationally separate from Justice McNeal LLC unless a formal structure is created that connects them.

### Property-Holding LLCs

Property-holding LLCs are planned separate entities that may each hold an individual property. This concept is intended to help organize ownership, separate liability, and simplify recordkeeping. Each property-holding LLC should maintain its own records, accounts, insurance, tax documents, and operating agreement when appropriate.

### Investment Accounts

Investment accounts may hold ETFs, cash reserves, and other long-term investment assets connected to the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission. Account titling, tax treatment, and trust connection should be reviewed by qualified professionals before being finalized.

### Future Entities

The family may create or connect additional future businesses, brands, digital assets, or asset-holding entities over time. Each entity should have a clear purpose and should not be automatically combined with Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, or property-holding LLCs unless formally connected by legal documents.

## 8. Contribution Flow

Contributions are voluntary funds contributed through Justice McNeal LLC in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission.

Contributions do not create ownership, equity, LLC membership, voting rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial returns.

Current contribution flow concept:

```text
Contributor
   ↓
Website / Stripe
   ↓
Justice McNeal LLC / Bluevine
   ↓
Fidelity or other approved account
   ↓
Mission-approved uses
```

Mission-approved uses may include long-term investing, administrative costs, legal and tax planning, website costs, family events connected to the mission, future trust setup, and other approved legacy-related purposes as described in the Charter, Contribution Policy, and related planning documents.

For detailed contribution rules, Active Legacy Contributor status, refunds, and payment handling, see the *Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation Contribution Policy*.

## 9. Asset Ownership Concept

The long-term goal is for major legacy assets to be owned by the intended future trust or by entities owned by the intended future trust, rather than being directly divided among individuals in a way that could become fragmented, sold, misused, or lost.

Property may be owned by a separate property-holding LLC, and that LLC may be owned by the intended future trust.

Example:

- **123 Tony Stark Road LLC** may own the property at 123 Tony Stark Road.
- **Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation**, as the intended future trust structure, may own **123 Tony Stark Road LLC**.

This example is for planning purposes only. The exact property ownership structure should be reviewed by an attorney, CPA, insurance professional, and real estate professional before being implemented.

## 10. Decision-Making Concept

### Current Decision-Making

Until the intended future trust and related governance documents are legally created, final decision-making authority for Justice McNeal LLC and activities administered through it in support of the Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation mission remains with Justin McNeal as owner and managing member of Justice McNeal LLC.

Justin McNeal may seek input from family members, Contributors, advisors, or appointed role-holders before making major decisions. Family participation, recommendations, contributions, or appointed roles do not create automatic voting rights, ownership rights, management rights, trustee rights, beneficiary rights, repayment rights, withdrawal rights, or guaranteed financial benefits unless otherwise documented in legally binding documents.

### Future Decision-Making

Once the intended future trust, updated LLC operating agreements, and formal governance documents are legally created, major decisions should follow the rules and procedures established in those documents.

Future concepts may include trustees, successor trustees, LLC managers, entity representatives, advisors, and family council roles. These roles are planning concepts only until they are defined and created through proper legal documents.

## 11. Entity Separation

Justice McNeal LLC, Karry Kraze, and future property-holding LLCs are separate entities with distinct purposes unless formally connected by legal documents.

Each entity should maintain proper records, accounts, and documentation when appropriate. Entities should not be treated as interchangeable, and assets should not be commingled across entities without proper structure and professional review.

Justice McNeal LLC is the current contribution and mission-administration vehicle. Karry Kraze is a separate e-commerce business. Property-holding LLCs are separate holding entities for individual properties. Investment accounts and future entities may serve their own distinct purposes within the broader legacy structure.

## 12. Professional Review Needed

This structure document should be reviewed by a qualified attorney, CPA, or other appropriate professional before being relied on for legal, tax, financial, estate planning, or governance decisions.

Questions that may require professional review include:

- Should the trust legally be named Justice McNeal Legacy Foundation, or would another name be clearer?
- Should the intended future trust own Justice McNeal LLC directly?
- How should Karry Kraze connect to the intended future trust—direct ownership, distributions, or another structure?
- Should each major property be held in its own LLC?
- How should contributions be classified and documented?
- How should Stripe, Bluevine, Fidelity, and investment accounts be titled?
- What tax, estate planning, securities, liability, and regulatory issues need review?
- How should trustees, managers, beneficiaries, and family roles be defined once the trust is created?
- How should records, reporting, and entity separation be maintained over time?

Professional review should be completed before this structure overview is used as the basis for formal legal documents, trust creation, LLC restructuring, asset transfers, or long-term governance.
