# ⭐ Justice McNeal LLC — Member Credit System

## Overview

The **Member Credit System** rewards responsible participation in the Justice McNeal Generational Wealth Plan.

Members earn credit points through positive financial behavior and active involvement in the family system.

Credit points determine a member's **Credit Tier**, which unlocks benefits such as:

- Higher borrowing limits
- Preferred loan interest rates
- Access to additional financial opportunities
- Recognition within the family system

The credit system encourages responsible participation while strengthening the overall financial health of the trust.

---

# Core Purpose

The Member Credit System exists to:

• Encourage consistent contributions  
• Reward responsible financial behavior  
• Protect the trust from lending risk  
• Promote long-term participation  
• Build a culture of financial discipline within the family

Rather than allowing unlimited borrowing, the credit system ensures that members earn greater financial flexibility through demonstrated responsibility.

---

# Credit Score Structure

Each member has an internal **Credit Score** within the system.

The score is calculated using multiple participation factors.

Example components may include:

| Factor | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Contribution History | Consistency of monthly payments |
| Contribution Amount | Total lifetime contributions |
| Membership Age | How long the member has been active |
| Loan Repayment | On-time repayment of loans |
| Participation | Involvement in the family system |

Credit scores update periodically as new activity occurs.

---

# Credit Tiers

Members are grouped into tiers based on their credit score.

| Tier | Borrow Limit | Description |
|----|---------------|-------------|
| Bronze | 15% | New or low participation |
| Silver | 20% | Consistent contributor |
| Gold | 25% | Long-term responsible member |
| Platinum | 30% | Highest trust level |

Borrow limits are calculated as a percentage of **total lifetime contributions**.

Example:


Total contributions: $10,000
Credit Tier: Gold
Borrow limit: 25%

Maximum loan: $2,500


---

# How Members Earn Credit Points

Members earn points through responsible participation.

### Consistent Contributions

Maintaining regular monthly contributions increases credit points.

Examples:

- Monthly contribution streaks
- No missed payments
- Long-term membership

---

### Lifetime Contributions

Members who invest larger amounts into the system gain additional credit points.

This rewards members who strengthen the family capital base.

---

### Loan Repayment Behavior

Members who take loans and repay them responsibly gain additional credit points.

Positive behaviors include:

- On-time payments
- Early repayment
- Completing loan terms without default

---

### Participation in the Family System

Future versions of the system may award points for participation such as:

- Attending family meetings
- Participating in governance votes
- Helping grow the membership network
- Contributing ideas to improve the system

---

# Credit Point Penalties

Members may lose credit points for behaviors that increase financial risk.

Examples include:

- Late loan payments
- Missed monthly contributions
- Loan default
- Violating system policies

Penalties help maintain financial discipline and protect the trust.

---

# Credit Tier Adjustments

Credit tiers may change over time based on activity.

Examples:

**Tier Upgrade**


Silver → Gold


Possible reasons:

- Long-term contribution streak
- Successful loan repayment
- Increased lifetime contributions

---

**Tier Downgrade**


Gold → Silver


Possible reasons:

- Missed loan payments
- Contribution inactivity
- Policy violations

---

Possible benefits include:

• Higher borrowing limits

As the trust grows, additional benefits may be added.
