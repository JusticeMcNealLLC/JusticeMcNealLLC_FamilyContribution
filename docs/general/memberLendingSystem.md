# 🏦 Justice McNeal LLC — Family Lending Program

## Overview

The **Family Lending Program** allows members of the Justice McNeal Generational Wealth Plan to borrow money from the family investment fund at fair, below-market interest rates.

Instead of members relying on traditional banks or credit cards with high interest rates, this system allows the family to **be its own bank**.

Interest paid on loans returns to the trust and helps grow the family investment fund, benefiting all members.

This creates a **self-reinforcing financial system** where:

• Members gain access to capital  
• The trust earns interest income  
• Family wealth grows over time  

---

# Core Principles

The Family Lending Program is built on five principles:

1. **Responsibility**  
   Loans must be repaid according to agreed terms.

2. **Fairness**  
   All members have equal access based on contributions and credit tier.

3. **Capital Protection**  
   The lending program must never endanger the long-term stability of the trust.

4. **Transparency**  
   All lending activity is recorded and visible through the member portal.

5. **Generational Sustainability**  
   The program must remain viable for future family members.

---

# Lending Pool Structure

The Justice McNeal Generational Wealth Plan divides capital into three internal pools:

### 1. Main Investment Pool
Purpose: Long-term wealth growth.

Assets typically include:

- VTI — Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF  
- VXUS — Vanguard Total International ETF  
- VIG — Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF  

Funds in this pool are **not used for loans**.

---

### 2. Loan / Liquidity Pool

Purpose: Provide capital for loans and operational liquidity.

Primary asset:

- SPAXX — Fidelity Government Money Market Fund

Uses:

• Member loans  
• Operational expenses  
• Insurance premiums  
• Emergency liquidity  

Target allocation:


25–40% of total trust value


Minimum threshold:


15% liquidity


If liquidity falls below the threshold, funds may be rebalanced from the investment pool.

---

### 3. Descendant Pool

Purpose: Preserve inheritance capital for future generations.

When a member passes away:


30% of insurance payout → Descendant Pool
70% → Main Investment Pool


Funds in this pool remain invested but can support loans for descendants when eligible.

---

# Loan Availability

The total amount of loans issued at any given time is limited.

Maximum outstanding loans:


30% of total trust value


Example:


Trust value: $100,000
Maximum loan pool: $30,000


This ensures the trust always maintains sufficient capital.

---

# Borrowing Eligibility

To apply for a loan, a member must:

• Be an active member  
• Maintain monthly contributions  
• Have no outstanding delinquent loans  
• Meet minimum credit tier requirements  

---

# Member Credit System

Members earn **credit points** within the system based on positive participation.

Credit tiers determine borrowing limits.

| Tier | Borrow Limit |
|-----|--------------|
| Bronze | 15% |
| Silver | 20% |
| Gold | 25% |
| Platinum | 30% |

Borrow limits are calculated as a percentage of **total lifetime contributions**.

Example:


Member contributions: $10,000
Gold tier borrowing limit (25%)

Loan limit = $2,500


---

# Credit Point Sources

Members earn credit through:

• Consistent monthly contributions  
• Long membership history  
• Responsible loan repayment  
• Participation in family initiatives  

Members may lose points for:

• Late loan payments  
• Missed contributions  
• Violations of lending agreements  

---

# Loan Application Process

Step 1 — Loan Request

Member submits loan request through the portal.

Required information:

• Requested amount  
• Purpose of loan  
• Desired repayment term  

---

Step 2 — Eligibility Check

The system automatically verifies:

• Borrow limit  
• Available loan pool funds  
• Member credit tier  

---

Step 3 — Administrative Review

Loans may require approval from the **Trust Admin or Lending Committee**.

Review considerations:

• Trust liquidity levels  
• Loan size relative to trust value  
• Member repayment history  

---

Step 4 — Loan Agreement

Once approved, the system generates a digital **Loan Agreement / Promissory Note**.

Agreement includes:

• Loan amount  
• Interest rate  
• Repayment schedule  
• Default terms  

Members must electronically sign the agreement.

---

Step 5 — Loan Disbursement

Funds are transferred to the member through:

• Bank transfer  
• ACH payment  
• Direct deposit

Loan balance becomes active within the system.

---

# Interest Rates

Interest rates are designed to remain **below typical bank rates** while still growing the trust.

Typical structure:


Family Loan Interest Rate
4% – 6%


Rates may vary depending on:

• Member credit tier  
• Loan size  
• Loan purpose  

Interest payments go directly back into the trust.

---

# Repayment Terms

Loans are repaid through scheduled monthly payments.

Repayment options may include:

• 12 months  
• 24 months  
• 36 months  

Members may repay early without penalty.

Early repayment increases credit score within the system.

---

# Late Payments

Late payments are handled with the following policy:

Grace period:


10 days


After the grace period:

• Late fee may be applied  
• Member credit score reduced  

Repeated late payments may result in:

• Suspension of borrowing privileges  
• Administrative review  

---

# Loan Default

A loan is considered in default if:

• Payments are overdue by 60+ days  
• Member fails to communicate repayment plan  

Default actions may include:

• Suspension of membership benefits  
• Structured repayment agreement  
• Legal recovery if necessary  

The goal is always **resolution, not punishment**.

---

# Transparency & Reporting

All lending activity is recorded in the system.

Members can view:

• Active loans  
• Loan balances  
• Repayment schedules  
• Interest paid to the trust  

Administrators can view:

• Total outstanding loans  
• Liquidity pool levels  
• Lending pool utilization
