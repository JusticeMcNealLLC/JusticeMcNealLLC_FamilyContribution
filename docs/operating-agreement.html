Article V — Allocations & Distributions

5.1 Profits and Losses. All profits, losses, and other tax items of the Company shall be allocated 100% to the Member.

5.2 Distributions. The Member may take distributions from the Company at any time and in any amount, provided that such distribution does not render the Company unable to pay its debts as they become due in the ordinary course of business.

5.3 Distribution Priority. Before any distribution, the Company should maintain adequate reserves for:

  1. Operating expenses (hosting, subscriptions, and service fees);
  2. Stripe processing fees and anticipated refunds;
  3. Quarterly estimated tax payments;
  4. Planned business investments; and
  5. A minimum operating reserve of at least one (1) month's operating expenses.

Article VI — Management

6.1 Member-Managed. The Company shall be member-managed. The Member shall have full and exclusive authority to manage the business and affairs of the Company.

6.2 Powers of the Member. The Member shall have the power to:

  1. Execute contracts, leases, and agreements on behalf of the Company;
  2. Open and manage bank accounts and brokerage accounts;
  3. Hire and terminate employees, contractors, and service providers;
  4. Make investment decisions for the Company;
  5. Acquire, hold, and dispose of property;
  6. Borrow money and grant security interests in Company assets;
  7. Commence or settle litigation;
  8. Take any and all actions necessary to carry out the Company's purpose.

6.3 Compensation. The Member may receive reasonable compensation for services rendered to the Company, as determined by the Member, in addition to any distributions.

6.4 Standard of Care. The Member shall manage the Company in good faith and with the care an ordinarily prudent person in a similar position would exercise under similar circumstances.

Article VIII — Tax Treatment

8.1 Tax Classification. The Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes. All income, deductions, and credits of the Company shall be reported on the Member's personal tax return (IRS Form 1040, Schedule C).

8.2 Tax Identification. The Company shall obtain its own Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the Internal Revenue Service. Until the EIN is obtained, the Member's Social Security Number may be used.

8.3 Tax Elections. The Member shall have sole authority to make all tax elections and decisions on behalf of the Company.

8.4 Self-Employment Tax. The Member acknowledges that net earnings from the Company are subject to self-employment tax under IRC § 1401, and the Member shall make quarterly estimated tax payments as required.

8.5 Georgia State Tax. The Company shall comply with all Georgia state tax obligations, including filing Georgia Form 700 (partnership return) if required, and paying Georgia income taxes through the Member's personal return.

Article IX — Liability & Indemnification

9.1 Limited Liability. The Member shall not be personally liable for any debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a member, except as otherwise provided by the Act.

9.2 Indemnification. The Company shall indemnify the Member and any agents of the Company to the fullest extent permitted by the Act against any and all losses, claims, damages, or expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from any action taken in good faith on behalf of the Company.

9.3 Insurance. The Company may, at its discretion, obtain and maintain liability insurance to protect the Company and its Member.

Article X — Transfer of Membership Interest

10.1 Restriction on Transfer. The Member may transfer all or any portion of the membership interest in the Company at any time and to any person or entity, subject to applicable law.

10.2 Admission of Transferee. A transferee of the membership interest shall become a substituted member only upon the written consent of the existing Member and execution of an amendment to this Agreement.

10.3 Transfer to Holding Entity. The Member anticipates that the Company may be transferred to or reorganized under Justice McNeal Holding LLC (or successor holding entity) in the future. Upon such transfer, this Agreement shall be amended accordingly.

Article XI — Dissolution

11.1 Events of Dissolution. The Company shall dissolve upon the occurrence of any of the following:

  1. The written decision of the Member to dissolve the Company;
  2. The sale or disposition of substantially all Company assets;
  3. The death or incapacity of the Member, unless a successor is designated; or
  4. Any event requiring dissolution under the Act.

11.2 Winding Up. Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up by the Member (or a designated representative). Company assets shall be distributed in the following order:

  1. Payment of debts and liabilities of the Company, including debts owed to the Member;
  2. Establishment of any reserves necessary for contingent or unforeseen liabilities;
  3. Distribution of the remaining assets to the Member.

11.3 Articles of Dissolution. Upon completion of winding up, articles of dissolution or termination shall be filed with the Georgia Secretary of State.

Article XII — Holding Company Integration

12.1 Future Restructuring. The Member intends to integrate the Company into a multi-entity structure under Justice McNeal Holding LLC (the "Holding Company"). Upon formation of the Holding Company, revenue from this Company may flow upstream to the Holding Company in accordance with an inter-company operating agreement.

12.2 Amendment Upon Restructuring. When the Company is brought under the Holding Company, this Agreement shall be amended or replaced with a multi-member or subsidiary operating agreement as appropriate. Any such restructuring shall be documented in writing.

12.3 Related Entities. The Member also operates or plans to operate the following entities under the Holding Company umbrella. This Company's operations shall remain separate from these entities:

  • [TBD] AI Content Generation LLC (AI content services)

Article XIII — Digital Assets & Intellectual Property

13.1 Ownership. All intellectual property created for or by the Company, including but not limited to source code, website designs, brand assets, logos, and written content, shall be the exclusive property of the Company.

13.2 Platform Assets. The following digital assets are owned by the Company:

  • The Family Contribution web application (source code repository);
  • Domain name(s) associated with the platform;
  • Supabase database and all stored data;
  • Stripe account and payment processing configuration;
  • All brand assets, including logos, design systems, and marketing materials;
  • GitHub repository and deployment infrastructure; and
  • All member data (subject to applicable privacy laws).

13.3 Licensing. The Member may license Company intellectual property to third parties or related entities with written documentation of the terms.

Article XIV — Privacy & Member Data

14.1 Data Responsibility. The Company collects and stores personal and financial data from family members who use the platform. The Company shall handle all data responsibly and in compliance with applicable federal and state privacy laws.

14.2 Data Retention. The Company shall maintain data only as long as necessary for business, legal, and tax purposes. Upon termination of a family member's account, their personal data shall be handled in accordance with the Company's privacy policy.

14.3 Security. The Company shall implement reasonable security measures, including but not limited to row-level security policies, encrypted storage, and access controls to protect member data.

Article XV — General Provisions

15.1 Entire Agreement. This Operating Agreement constitutes the entire agreement regarding the Company and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous agreements, understandings, or representations.

15.2 Amendment. This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by the Member.

15.3 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Georgia, without regard to conflict of law principles.

15.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

15.5 Headings. Section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

15.6 Binding Effect. This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Member and the Member's heirs, executors, administrators, successors, and assigns.
